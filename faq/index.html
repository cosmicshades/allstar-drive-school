FAQ

Frequently Asked Driving Questions

We feel that it is important to provide a resource to answer any driving questions that a parent or driver may ask. This is a constantly growing resource, for both All Star Drive School related questions and questions related to drivers education or driving laws.

Can my student drive siblings?

Once the student has received their license, they may drive their siblings.

Can my student drive out of state?

Any type of license can be taken out of state! Just follow the state rules.

When can they take the driving test?

They can take the test at any time after completion of drivers education!

Can the driving test be taken at the drive school?

Absolutely! All Star is state certified to give tests. Advantages of taking it at the drive school are your student is familiar with the car, the car doesn’t need to be inspected (drive school provides) and the student is familiar with their driver instructor. However, there is an additional charge of $30 for the drive test.

When is my child eligible to enroll?

If your student is enrolled in drivers education at All Star and places a $100 deposit, they can go to the BMV with a slip issued from All Star driving school when they are 15 years old.

Can my child learn on a stick shift?

All Star does not teach stick shift driving, and all cars are on automatic transmission.

How are times setup for driving hours?

Students pick available times, and can choose when and with whom to drive.

Can sessions be mixed and matched?

You may attend days in multiple sessions, including Sundays, just so long as the requirements for the class are met. Make arrangements with All Star to make this possible. This is great for families who wish to travel and will miss a day of driver’s ed instruction.

Have other questions?

We strive to have the best customer satisfaction, so if you have any unanswered questions, don’t hesitate to email us at allstardriveschool@gmail.com
